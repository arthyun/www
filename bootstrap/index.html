Sustainability

Globally, electric mobility is the key to sustainable and environmentally friendly mobility.

This is why Audi wants to make our goal of introducing a pure electric vehicle with zero CO2 emissions by 2025.
For us, electric mobility is an essential component of creating an eco-friendly and sustainable transportation system.

Electric mobility for long distance driving in cooperation with IONITY

With the launch of its first all-electric model, the e-tron, Audi launched a home charging system and an external charging solution. Building a charging network is essential for long-distance driving of electric vehicles.

Audi is participating in IONITY's electric vehicle super-fast charging business, and is contributing to the expansion of the external charging system. IONITY is building a charging network on European roads and plans to install around 400 Fast Charging Stations (HPCs) in 25 countries by 2020. The Audi e-tron charging service allows customers to use the IONITY network at a discounted rate and access most charging facilities in Europe with one card.

New TFSI e model line

Among Audi models, e-tron refers to pure electric vehicles, and plug-in models are called “TFSI e”. The plug-in hybrid model can be driven in two ways. For short distances, electric driving using a compact battery is possible, and for long-distance driving, the use of an internal combustion engine is possible.

Quiet running

While driving an electric vehicle in the city center, it is possible to drive quietly enough to not even feel the noise from the outside as well as the inside. Even while driving at high speed, only the wind and road noise are heard. There is no carbon emission generated during driving.

Worry free mileage

Switch on the engine. At first it works as an auxiliary role for the electric motor and completely converts it to an internal combustion engine operation, which ensures a sufficient driving range. While stopped, you can refuel as usual and continue driving.
