Synopsis

A perfect assassin. An innocent girl. They have nothing left to lose except each other. Killer Leon, who wanders without roots with a bag with two milk packs in one hand and a flowerpot in the other. One day, he witnesses the genocide of the girl next door, Matilda's family. Meanwhile, Matilda returns from an errand and asks Leon for help when her family is slaughtered. Matilda, a 12-year-old girl, determined to become a killer to avenge her family. Instead of teaching Leon to write, he learns to take revenge. Finally, she learns that the person who killed her family is a corrupt drug cop. I'm heading to his house...

A 1994 French film directed by Luc Besson. It is a story of revenge between a girl who has lost her parents and a knowledgeable killer. The French director Besson's challenge to Hollywood is regarded as a masterpiece with both the fun as an action movie and the pity as a melodrama. In particular, after the release, conservative critics gave a harsh evaluation, but as a result, it not only made a big box office profit in the US market, but also achieved tremendous success in Korea and other countries, causing the Leon Syndrome.
